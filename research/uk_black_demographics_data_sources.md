# UK Black Demographics — Verified Data Sources Reference

> **Purpose:** Comprehensive catalogue of citation-quality UK data sources for a Black British demographics platform, modelled on blackdemographics.com.
>
> **Standard:** All sources listed are official government statistics, National Statistics-badged publications, or established academic surveys that would pass citation in an official government report or graduate-level academic work.
>
> **Compiled:** March 2025

## 3. ECONOMICS

### 3.1 Employment

| Source | Publisher | URL | Detail |
|--------|-----------|-----|--------|
| **Ethnicity Facts & Figures — Employment** | Cabinet Office / RDU | `ethnicity-facts-figures.service.gov.uk/work-pay-and-benefits/employment/employment/latest` | Employment rate (%) for 16-64 year olds. 18-group ethnicity (Black African, Black Caribbean, Black Other). By sex, age, disability. Based on Annual Population Survey. Annual. England & Wales. |
| **ONS A09 — Labour Market Status by Ethnic Group** | ONS | `ons.gov.uk/employmentandlabourmarket/peopleinwork/employmentandemployeetypes/datasets/labourmarketstatusbyethnicgroupa09` | Employment level/rate, economically active/inactive, full-time/part-time. 5-group ethnicity (sometimes 18-group). | **Ethnicity Facts & Figures — Employment by Occupation** | Cabinet Office / RDU | `ethnicity-facts-figures.service.gov.uk/workforce-and-business/workforce-diversity/employment-by-occupation/latest` | Employment by SOC major groups (managers, professionals, skilled trades, etc.) × ethnicity. 18-group. Based on APS. |
| **Nomis APS `NM_17_5` — Variables (percentages)** | ONS via Nomis | `nomisweb.co.uk/datasets/apsnew` | Current machine-readable APS route used for the live economics page. UK labour-market rates, regional comparisons, and occupation shares. Caveat: for the live percentage series, these measures currently expose a broad `Black or Black British` group rather than separate Black African, Black Caribbean, and Other Black rates. |

### 3.2 Unemployment

| Source | Publisher | URL | Detail |
|--------|-----------|-----|--------|
| **Ethnicity Facts & Figures — Unemployment** | Cabinet Office / RDU | `ethnicity-facts-figures.service.gov.uk/work-pay-and-benefits/unemployment-and-economic-inactivity/unemployment/latest` | ILO unemployment rate by 18-group ethnicity, sex, age. Based on APS. Annual. |
| **ONS A09 Dataset** | ONS | Same URL as employment A09 above | Unemployment level/rate by ethnicity and sex. Quarterly. UK-wide. |

> **LIVE IMPLEMENTATION NOTE (2026-03-10):** The first public `/economics` route currently uses Nomis APS `NM_17_5` because it is official, current, and machine-readable. That means the live labour-market slice is broad-group only for now and does **not** yet recover separate Black African, Black Caribbean, and Other Black rates.

### 3.3 Black-Owned Businesses

| Source | Publisher | URL | Detail |
|--------|-----------|-----|--------|
| **Ethnicity Facts & Figures — Self-Employment** | Cabinet Office / RDU | `ethnicity-facts-figures.service.gov.uk/workforce-and-business/business/self-employment/latest` | Self-employment rate by 18-group ethnicity and sex. Based on APS. Annual. **Best available proxy for Black business ownership.** |
| **Longitudinal Small Business Survey (LSBS)** | DBT (formerly BEIS) | `gov.uk/government/collections/small-business-survey-reports` | Business characteristics of "Minority Ethnic Led" (MEL) businesses: sector, size, turnover, exporting, innovation, access to finance. ~9,000-11,000 SMEs surveyed. Annual. UK-wide. |

> **MAJOR GAP:** The UK has **no official register of businesses by owner ethnicity**. The IDBR (ONS), Companies House, and HMRC do not record ethnicity. The number of "Black-owned businesses" cannot be directly counted from any official source. Self-employment rate is the best proxy. The LSBS uses only aggregate "Minority Ethnic Led" — not Black-specific.

### 5.1 Health Status & Life Expectancy

| Source | Publisher | URL | Detail |
|--------|-----------|-----|--------|
| **Census 2021 — General Health by Ethnic Group** | ONS (via Ethnicity Facts & Figures) | `ethnicity-facts-figures.service.gov.uk/health/physical-health/general-health/latest` | Self-reported health (very good → very bad) by 19-category ethnicity. Census 2021. |

## 7. KNOWN DATA GAPS

These are structural gaps where the UK system does not collect or publish ethnicity data in ways comparable to the US:

| Gap | Impact | Best Available Proxy |
|-----|--------|---------------------|
| **Ethnicity not on death certificates** (England & Wales) | No routine life expectancy by ethnicity | ONS Census-linkage (2011-2014 only); Census 2021 self-reported health |
| **Ethnicity not on marriage registrations** | No marriage/divorce rates by ethnicity | Census snapshot of marital status; APS |
| **No business register records owner ethnicity** | Cannot count Black-owned businesses | Self-employment rates (APS); LSBS aggregate "Minority Ethnic Led" |
| **No official dataset of MPs/councillors by ethnicity** | Political representation data is secondary | Commons Library briefing; LGA councillor survey |
| **Immigration data by nationality, not ethnicity** | Cannot directly identify "Black immigration" | Census country of birth × ethnicity cross-tabulation |
| **Scotland Census delayed & quality issues** | UK-wide figures incomplete | NRS Scotland's Census 2022 (data quality under review) |
| **Wealth data from 2016-18** | Most current comprehensive ethnicity × wealth data is dated | WAS Round 9 may update; Understanding Society for longitudinal |

## 9. ETHNICITY CLASSIFICATION NOTES

Different sources use different classification schemes. Key for Black demographics:

### Census 2021 (England & Wales) — 19 categories
Under **"Black, Black British, Black Welsh, Caribbean or African"**:
- African
- Caribbean
- Other Black

Under **"Mixed or Multiple ethnic groups"** (important — often overlooked):
- White and Black Caribbean
- White and Black African

### DfE Education Data — ~19 extended categories
- Black African
- Black Caribbean
- Any Other Black Background
- White and Black Caribbean ← **highest exclusion rates in England**
- White and Black African

### Police / CJS Data — Officer-perceived 5+1
- White, Black, Asian, Mixed, Other, Not stated
- Self-identified 16+1 available in some datasets

### Survey Data (FRS, EHS, WAS, HSE) — typically 5-group
- White, Mixed, Asian, Black, Other
- Small samples often prevent further disaggregation

> **Critical:** "Black" is not monolithic in UK data. Black Caribbean, Black African, and Other Black show **markedly different outcomes** across education, employment, health, and criminal justice. The Mixed White and Black Caribbean group is analytically essential. Always seek the most disaggregated data available.
